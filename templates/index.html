Why is Model Accuracy at Random-Guess Levels?

A key finding from exploring the Customer Support Ticket dataset is that the labels for Ticket Type and Ticket Priority are completely synthetic and lack correlation with the actual semantic content of the ticket descriptions. This represents a classic real-world data science scenario where:

  • Data labels are misaligned or randomly generated in database records.
  • Standard algorithms like LinearSVC achieve baseline random accuracy (20-25%).
  • The system operates as a decision-support foundation, routing cases while highlighting how operational processes require robust and clean ground-truth labeling to build higher fidelity models.
